The Faunadex

Seviper vs. Zangoose

Seviper

Seviper, the Fang Snake Pokemon is a poison type that resembles a viper. Seviper's swordlike tail serves two purposes - it slashes foes and douses them with secreted poison. This Pokémon will not give up its long-running blood feud with Zangoose.

Viper

The Viperidae (vipers) are a family of venomous snakes found in most parts of the world, with the exception of Antarctica, Australia, Hawaii, Madagascar, as well as various other isolated islands, and north of the Arctic Circle. They have longer fangs in comparison to other snakes that are hinged to allow deep penetration. Vipers are carnivores that eat small mammals, birds, lizards, and frogs but are predated on by larger mammals and birds.

Zangoose

Zangoose, the Cat Ferret Pokemon, is a normal type based off of a mongoose despite the latter moniker. Zangoose usually stays on all fours, but when angered, it gets up on its hind legs and extends its claws. This Pokémon shares a bitter rivalry with Seviper that dates back over generations.

Mongoose

The mongoose is a small terrestrial carnivore that has species native to southern Europe, Africa, and Asia. They feed mostly on insects, crabs, earthworms, lizards, birds, and rodents. They can be semi-domesticated and used to control vermin. The Buddhist god of wealth Vaiśravaṇa, is frequently depicted holding a mongoose that is spitting jewels from its mouth.
